Monetization strategies

These applications are really hilarious and if you add some nice features, you’ll get an army of loyal fans. But what’s next? How to make an app make money for you? Here are some great ideas how you can monetize your app and become a little bit richer (or far richer).
Paid application. It's not necessary to make your application free. It can cost a dollar or $1,99 or 5 or ten bucks. Paid applications are very popular as well and even the fact that the market has some free competitors doesn’t mean that no one would buy your application. That's not true. Of course, a free app has more chances to be installed but it will bring less money than the paid one. If you think that a small fee for your app is ok, then you have to think through a few issues.
  • First of all, your has to offer really nice features to make the customers interested. If your application will offer nothing new and there are many similar free apps, then you’ve got a problem.
  • Second of all, paid apps are preferable for iOS platform, because this audience is used to paying for services.
  • Third of all, you need to work hard on the marketing strategy of your app and advertise it in such a way that everyone would fancy it before the release.
In-app-purchase. Is probably one of the most popular ways to monetize prank apps. In such a case, some features should be free and some (the most interesting ones) could be paid. Here is a pitfall you need to overcome. The free functionality has to be interesting and funny as well as paid features. If the most interesting things would be free then why would customers pay?
Freemium. It’s a great model to make money with your application. But here goes the same issue as with in-app-purchase. If free version is dull it will be deleted and no one cares that the greatest and the funniest pranks are in the premium account. And vice versa, if the most interesting part is free users would not see the point to pay for premium account.
Advertisements. You can make your app absolutely free for users (they would appreciate it) adding some pop-ups or banners. Modern customers don’t actually notice these ads because they are everywhere, so we tend to ignore them. At the same time, it is a great way to monetize your app, not at users’ expense. It would be great if these apps are close to the context of your app or they advertise similar applications. In such a case, your users would not be very annoyed with it.
Subscription. To offer this monetization strategy to your users you have to be (brave) sure that your app is worth it. It has to be not only unique and super interesting application but something more. You will need to work hard and supply users with new functionality with each update. Frankly speaking, subscription works well only if the context of an application updates all the time and users know that they would get something fresh and great each month or so.
