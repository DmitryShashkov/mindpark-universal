W recie in’t enh inets

As the cybercrime industry is huge and the number of incidents is growing, companies can’t be 100% safe from security threats. What can and should be done is minimizing risks and, consequently, damage, by taking security seriously. Yet, a lot of companies still rely on a reactive approach. But is it safe enough? It’s time to find out.
Reactive approach teaches us to clean mess up after the attack has happened. Following the logic of this method, you have to collect all the information about an attack after it has been committed. Then, you need to analyse this data and find out the weaknesses in your defence system. After that, you fix the defence system. Such a method has a bunch of downsides, here are the most obvious:
Data leaks. The majority of modern cybersecurity solutions use the scheme which is based on the three main stages - react, respond and recover. Which leads to inevitable data leaks. Meanwhile, business owners have to deal with furious customers, and partners trying to explain how their information got to the public access.
Out-of-date protection. While you are dealing with some old issues patching up the system, you are absolutely open to the new threads. Just like real viruses, virtual ones mutate as well. They adapt to the new antiviruses and learn how to trick your protection.
Other risks ignorance. Once you’ve been hurt, you are trying to cure yourself of a wound. You want to protect yourself from this thread in the future and ignore your back. One of the main reasons for cyber attacks are people, the employees of a company who opened a malware file or installed a program with a virus.
Security management is a complex systematic process, dealing with all aspects of company’s activities, starting from personnel employment and termination to vendor management, from security equipment implementation and secure software development to business continuity management, from data backup to event monitoring and incident response. That is why apart from the reactive approach we need to implement the proactive method.
How to shift to proactive approach
Proactive approach is based on the idea of prevention. You detect all the defects in your cybersecurity system and fix them before a real thread appears. In such a mode, you provide your customers with a much higher level of protection and exclude a data breach risk.

Once you’ve realized that proactive approach helps to protect your company and all your customers, you need to start its implementation. First, and the most important step is a mental shift which you need to accept. Usually, company hosts ignore the approach because it requires larger investments than the reactive method. Still, in a long-term perspective, it allows you to save millions of dollars and your reputation as well. Here are the most important rules and steps you need to take into account building your cybersecurity strategy:
Educate your staff. The majority of all cyber attacks were successful only due to the human errors and negligence. Unknown files and emails, unverified applications, too simple passwords, unknown flash drives and general carelessness make your company and information it holds vulnerable to hacker attacks.
What you can do. First of all, explain your staff what information they are responsible for and that their action can lead to adverse consequences and data leaks. Second of all, you need to draw up a non-disclosure agreement and ask your employees to sign it. Anf third of all, education is a process that is why such events as conferences and lectures on the cybersecurity topic. All these measures should teach your employees to be more responsible and self-disciplined.
